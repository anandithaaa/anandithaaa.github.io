About

I'm a senior undergraduate at NYU Courant Institute studying Math & Computer Science. I really love thinking about problems at the intersection of computer vision + learning + people. I firmly believe that we can broaden access and improve quality of care by integrating more vision and learning technologies into the healthcare system. This is the space I hope to impact through my research work!

I'm a grateful recipient of the NYU WinS scholarship as well as the Presidential Honors scholarship. I've won 4 DURF grants to pursue undergraduate research at NYU. I used to be an NYU College Leader and was awarded an President's Service award in 2018. Some more about me can be found in the school paper.

Legal Aid Society

Freshman spring I worked on a team at the Legal Aid Society to reengineer code to a proprietary forensic software used to determine guilt criminal trials within NY state and worldwide. Lawyers at Legal Aid used the work to get the tool's use outlawed in NY State through proving the existence of bias in the algorithmic methodology. The work was featured in the New York Law Journal.
